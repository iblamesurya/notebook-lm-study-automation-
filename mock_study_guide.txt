Quantum Computing Study Guide
Quantum computing is a rapidly-emerging technology that harnesses the laws of quantum mechanics to solve problems too complex for classical computers.
Today, IBM, Google, and other companies are building quantum hardware that can be programmed for various experimental and educational use cases.
The basic unit of quantum information is the qubit. Unlike a classical bit, which can only be 0 or 1, a qubit can exist in a superposition of states.
This allows quantum computers to process massive amounts of possibilities simultaneously.
Another key concept is quantum entanglement, which enables qubits that are entangled to share information instantaneously regardless of distance.
