# Quantum Flashcards

## Card 1

**Q:** What field of physics provides the foundational laws used in quantum computing?

**A:** Quantum mechanics

---

## Card 2

**Q:** Quantum computing is specifically designed to solve problems that are too _____ for classical computers.

**A:** complex

---

## Card 3

**Q:** Which two companies are specifically mentioned as builders of programmable quantum hardware?

**A:** IBM and Google

---

## Card 4

**Q:** What are the two primary use cases for the quantum hardware currently being developed?

**A:** Experimental and educational use cases

---

## Card 5

**Q:** What is the name of the basic unit of quantum information?

**A:** The qubit

---

## Card 6

**Q:** How many states can a classical bit represent at any single time?

**A:** One (either $0$ or $1$)

---

## Card 7

**Q:** Unlike a classical bit, a qubit can exist in a _____ of states.

**A:** superposition

---

## Card 8

**Q:** What is the primary processing advantage provided by the superposition of qubits?

**A:** It allows for the simultaneous processing of massive amounts of possibilities.

---

## Card 9

**Q:** Concept: Quantum entanglement

**A:** Definition: A state where qubits share information instantaneously regardless of the distance between them.

---

## Card 10

**Q:** Under what condition can qubits share information instantaneously across any distance?

**A:** When the qubits are entangled

---

## Card 11

**Q:** How does distance affect the transfer of information between entangled qubits?

**A:** Information is shared instantaneously regardless of distance.
